Introduction to Ethiopia

Ethiopia, officially known as the Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia, is a landlocked country located in the Horn of Africa. It is the second-most populous nation on the African continent, bordered by Eritrea to the north, Djibouti and Somalia to the east, Sudan and South Sudan to the west, and Kenya to the south.

Ethiopia has a rich history dating back thousands of years, with archaeological findings suggesting that it was one of the earliest regions to be inhabited by humans. The country is renowned for its diverse cultural heritage, ancient civilizations, and unique traditions.

With a landscape characterized by highlands, plateaus, and deep valleys, Ethiopia boasts stunning natural beauty and biodiversity. The Great Rift Valley, which traverses the country from northeast to south, is a prominent geographical feature.

Ethiopia's economy is predominantly agrarian, with agriculture being the mainstay of its economy. The country is known for its coffee production, with Ethiopian coffee being highly prized globally. In recent years, Ethiopia has also seen significant growth in other sectors such as manufacturing and services.
